# IMPORTANT CORRECTION — FORCE MUST BE SPLIT

A single "force" axis is not enough.

Why?

Because:

- `could` and `might` feel weak in DIFFERENT ways.
- `could` weakens by opening many possibilities.
- `might` weakens by lowering speaker commitment.

These are different cognitive operations.

---

# Replace FORCE with TWO AXES

## 1. POSSIBILITY SPACE WIDTH

How many worlds/scenarios remain open.

```text
closed ← narrow ← medium ← wide ← maximally open
```

Interpretation:

- high width = many scenarios remain possible
- low width = modal strongly constrains reality

---

## 2. EPISTEMIC COMMITMENT

How strongly the speaker believes the proposition.

```text
none ← weak ← medium ← high ← maximal
```

Interpretation:

- low commitment = "I'm not really claiming this"
- high commitment = "I strongly believe this"

---

# THIS FIXES could vs might

| Modal | POSSIBILITY WIDTH | COMMITMENT |
|---|---|---|
| could | VERY HIGH | VERY LOW |
| might | MEDIUM | LOW-MEDIUM |

---

# Meaning difference

## could

```text
the scenario is compatible with reality
```

The speaker does NOT strongly lean toward it.

It merely survives consistency checks.

---

## might

```text
I genuinely consider this scenario plausible
```

Now the speaker weakly commits.

---

# Compare

## could

```text
He could be home.
```

Meaning:

```text
nothing rules this out
```

---

## might

```text
He might be home.
```

Meaning:

```text
I think this may actually be true
```

---

# WHY could OFTEN FEELS WEAKER

Because:

```text
possibility ≠ belief
```

Example:

```text
He could be lying.
```

=

```text
lying is compatible with observations
```

---

```text
He might be lying.
```

=

```text
lying feels like a plausible explanation
```
